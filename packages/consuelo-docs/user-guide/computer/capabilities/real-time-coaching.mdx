## Overview

Real-time coaching transforms how your team handles calls. Instead of guessing what to say, reps get AI-powered suggestions that appear exactly when needed — based on what the customer is actually saying.

## How It Works

### 1. Call Detection

Computer automatically detects when you're on a call:

- Monitors your active call status from the dialer
- Loads context as soon as the call connects
- Recognizes when a call ends and switches to post-call analysis

### 2. Context Loading

Before coaching begins, Computer loads everything relevant:

| Context | What It Pulls                           |
| ------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Contact | Name, title, company, phone, email      |
| Deal    | Stage, amount, probability, close date  |
| History | Previous calls, notes, emails, meetings |
| Company | Industry, size, tech stack, revenue     |

This context shapes every suggestion Computer makes.

### 3. Live Transcription

The conversation is transcribed in real-time:

- Speaker diarization (who said what)
- Timestamps for key moments
- Sentiment tracking throughout the call

### 4. Suggestion Engine

Based on the transcript and context, Computer generates:

**Actionable Phrases**

Specific things to say right now:

> "Based on their objection about price, suggest: 'I understand budget is a concern. Many of our customers in [industry] found that the ROI paid for itself within 3 months. Would it help to see a case study?'"

**Pain Funnel Questions**

Questions to uncover needs:

> "Ask: 'What's the biggest challenge you're facing with your current solution?' followed by 'How is that affecting your team's productivity?'"

**Objection Handling**

When the customer pushes back:

> Customer objection: "We're happy with our current vendor"
>
> Suggested response: "That's great to hear. Out of curiosity, what do they do really well? And is there anything you wish they could improve?"

## Coaching Modes

### Standard Coaching

For regular calls, Computer provides:

- Phrase suggestions every 30-60 seconds
- Objection handling when detected
- Questions to uncover needs
- Closing signals and next steps

### Discovery Mode

When learning about a prospect:

- Focus on open-ended questions
- Pain funnel techniques
- Competitor research prompts
- Decision-maker identification

### Closing Mode

When a deal is near close:

- Trial close questions
- Urgency language
- Contract and timeline discussion
- Stakeholder alignment

## Tips for Best Results

### Speak Clearly

Clear speech improves transcription accuracy. Use a quality headset and minimize background noise.

### Let the Customer Finish

Wait for customer responses before your next question. Computer updates suggestions based on their answers.

### Use Suggested Phrases Naturally

The suggestions are starting points — adapt them to your voice and conversation style.

### Follow the Pain Funnel

Start broad, then go deeper:

1. "What challenges are you facing?"
2. "How does that affect your team?"
3. "What happens if you don't solve it?"
4. "How would solving this impact your business?"

### Watch for Buying Signals

Computer highlights when customers show interest:

- Asking about implementation
- Discussing timelines
- Mentioning budget approval
- Asking about next steps
