# research

Use for research lessons, research reports, source-grounded explainers, paper walkthroughs, and daily deep ideas.

## job

Turn source material into a durable teaching/report artifact that preserves evidence while improving understanding.

## basis

A strong research guide should make the research question, evidence, result, limitations, and implications easy to inspect. It should connect the paper's contribution back to the larger question, name limitations, compare or situate the result when relevant, and end with what the reader should now understand or do.

## required structure

1. **Hero**: topic, one-sentence thesis, source status, and reading time.
2. **Source card**: title, authors, year, publication/source, link, source type, and evidence status.
3. **Why this matters**: the real-world intuition or decision this source improves.
4. **Explain simply**: story, toy example, or metaphor before technical detail.
5. **Vocabulary**: compact cards for terms the reader needs before the walkthrough.
6. **Walkthrough**: question, method, result, importance, limitations, and the most important mechanism.
7. **Evidence / citations**: preserve citations and source links exactly. Do not drop factual anchors.
8. **Intuition builder**: technology, systems/business, and everyday-life examples.
9. **Deeper layer**: one advanced insight that goes beyond the simple explanation.
10. **Memory card**: 3-5 claims worth saving.
11. **Question**: one sharp question for Ko.

## html interaction pattern

Use HTML to make the research navigable, not decorative. Prefer a two-page or multi-panel reading surface with smooth-scroll section navigation, collapsible deep dives, tabbed examples or source views, hover/click glossary aids, and one lightweight interactive metaphor when it clarifies the core idea. The page should still be readable as static HTML if scripts fail.

## visual pattern

- Editorial white canvas with strong title hierarchy.
- Source card near the top, visibly separate from interpretation.
- Glossary as compact cards.
- Key result as numbered cards.
- Limitations as a sober callout.
- Final question as a strong closing panel.

## quality bar

- The guide should feel like a clear research mentor, not a paper summary dump.
- Start simple, then get rigorous.
- Preserve uncertainty and limitations.
- Do not invent research claims or citations.
- Use source material as truth; use design only to improve comprehension.
