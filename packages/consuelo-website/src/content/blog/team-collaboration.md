## The hidden cost of proprietary CRMs

| CRM | per seat/month | 20 agents/year |
|---|---|---|
| Salesforce | $150-300 | $36,000-72,000 |
| HubSpot Sales | $90-150 | $21,600-36,000 |
| Close.com | $99-139 | $23,760-33,360 |

and that's before add-ons, integrations, and API limits.

## What "open source" actually means

- **you own the code** — fork it, modify it, extend it
- **you own the data** — no vendor lock-in, export anytime
- **no per-seat fees** — host it yourself, pay for infrastructure
- **community-driven** — bugs get fixed by people who use it

> open source doesn't mean "free and unsupported."
> it means you're not dependent on one company's roadmap.

## What agencies actually need

| feature | do you need it? |
|---|---|
| contact management | yes |
| pipeline tracking | yes |
| call logging | yes |
| email sequences | maybe |
| AI lead scoring | nice to have |
| 500+ integrations | probably not |

most agencies use **10% of their CRM's features** and pay for 100%.

## The migration concern

> "switching CRMs sounds like a nightmare."

it doesn't have to be.

- **CSV import** — bring your contacts in minutes
- **API access** — sync with existing tools
- **gradual rollout** — run both systems in parallel

## Why now?

three things changed:

1. **AI got cheap** — coaching and analytics that used to cost $500/seat are now open source
2. **hosting got simple** — one-click deploys on railway, render, or your own server
3. **the tools caught up** — open source CRMs now match proprietary ones feature-for-feature
