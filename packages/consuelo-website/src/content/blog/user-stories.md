## Table of contents

## The metrics hierarchy

not all metrics are equal. here's how to think about them:

| tier | metric | why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **outcome** | revenue per agent | the only metric that pays bills |
| **conversion** | appointments per dial | efficiency of your process |
| **activity** | dials per hour | effort and tooling |
| **quality** | talk-to-listen ratio | skill and coaching |

> most agencies obsess over **activity metrics** and ignore everything above them.

## Activity metrics

these are the easiest to measure and the least useful alone.

- **dials per day** — how many calls did the agent make?
- **talk time** — how many minutes on the phone?
- **voicemails left** — are they actually reaching people?

**the trap:** rewarding dial volume without measuring conversation quality.

## Conversion metrics

this is where the real signal lives.

| metric | good | great |
|---|---|---|
| pickup rate | 8-12% | 15%+ |
| conversation to appointment | 15-20% | 25%+ |
| appointment to close | 20-30% | 35%+ |

if your pickup rate is low, **it's a dialer problem** (local presence, call timing).

if your conversation-to-appointment rate is low, **it's a coaching problem.**

## Quality metrics

harder to measure. more important.

- **talk-to-listen ratio** — top performers listen 60%+ of the time
- **question frequency** — good agents ask more questions
- **objection handling** — how quickly do they recover?
- **compliance score** — are they staying within guidelines?

> AI coaching tracks all of these automatically. no manual call reviews needed.

## The framework

1. **start with outcomes** — what's your revenue per agent?
2. **trace backwards** — which conversion step is the bottleneck?
3. **fix the bottleneck** — tooling for activity, coaching for quality
4. **measure again** — did the change move the outcome metric?

## Common mistakes

| mistake | why it's wrong |
|---|---|
| tracking only dials | activity without quality is noise |
| monthly reviews | too slow — problems compound daily |
| manual call scoring | only covers 5% of calls |
| same targets for all agents | new agents need different benchmarks |
