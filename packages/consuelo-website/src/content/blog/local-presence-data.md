[software advice research](https://readymode.com/local-presence-dialing/) found that **27.5%** of people answer a call from an unknown local number, compared to just **7%** for toll-free. that's a 4x difference from changing one thing: the area code.

## Table of contents

## The pickup rate breakdown

| caller ID type | avg answer rate |
|---|---|
| unknown toll-free (800) | 4-7% |
| unknown out-of-state | 5-8% |
| unknown local area code | **12-27.5%** |
| known/saved number | 80%+ |

sources: [Landbase](https://landbase.com/blog/direct-dial-coverage-statistics), [Koncert](https://www.koncert.com/blog/best-local-presence-dialer-software), [ReadyMode](https://readymode.com/local-presence-dialing/)

[94% of people](https://landbase.com/blog/direct-dial-coverage-statistics) believe unidentified calls are fraudulent. a local number doesn't guarantee a pickup — but it's the minimum viable trust signal.

## The math for a real team

take 20 agents making 200 dials/day each — 4,000 total dials daily.

| | without local presence | with local presence |
|---|---|---|
| daily conversations | 280 | **880** |
| monthly conversations | 5,600 | **17,600** |
| monthly appointments (15% rate) | 840 | **2,640** |

same team. same hours. **3x more conversations.**

## Cold calling in 2026

cold calling isn't dead — [69% of buyers](https://www.cloudtalk.io/blog/cold-calling-statistics) are open to cold calls, and 82% will take a meeting if the rep proves value. cognism analyzed 200K+ real calls and found the average success rate is [2.7%](https://scrap.io/cold-calling-success-rate) — but top performers hit **11.3%**. that 4x gap is mostly tooling and technique.

key stats from the data:

| metric | number |
|---|---|
| attempts to reach decision-maker | **8** |
| best time to call | **4-5pm midweek** |
| coaching impact on conversion | **+38%** |
| coaching impact on revenue/rep | **+50%** |

source: [Cleverly](https://cleverly.co/blog/cold-calling-statistics)

most reps quit after 2-3 attempts. the data says you need 8.

## The nuance: it depends on your audience

[nooks.ai analyzed 1.5M dials](https://nooks.ai/blog-posts/should-your-team-be-using-local-presence-in-2023) and found "little to no correlation" between local presence and connect rates — but their data is tech SDRs calling business prospects. insurance is different. you're calling consumers, not CTOs. the [GHL data](https://help.gohighlevel.com/support/solutions/articles/155000006736-local-presence-dialing-in-the-mobile-app) consistently shows 3-4x improvement for consumer-facing calls.

context matters. know your audience.

## The spam problem

local presence alone isn't enough in 2026. [aloware calls it](https://aloware.com/blog/how-to-double-your-call-pickup-rates) a "zero-trust world." you also need STIR/SHAKEN compliance, number rotation, reputation monitoring, and clean lists.

local presence gets you in the door. number hygiene keeps you there.
