## Table of contents

## The problem

most sales coaching happens **after the call ends.**

by then, the deal is already lost.

> the best coaches listen live and intervene in the moment.
> but you can't put a manager on every call.

## How real-time coaching works

| what happens | when |
|---|---|
| agent starts a call | coaching session begins |
| AI listens to both sides | continuous |
| objection detected | prompt appears instantly |
| compliance risk flagged | alert in real-time |
| call ends | summary + score generated |

the AI doesn't replace your manager. it **scales them** across every agent, every call.

## What agents see during a call

- **talk-to-listen ratio** — are you talking too much?
- **objection prompts** — suggested responses in real-time
- **compliance alerts** — flagged language before it's a problem
- **sentiment tracking** — is the prospect engaged or checked out?

all of this appears in a sidebar. no tab switching. no distractions.

## Before vs after

| metric | before AI coaching | after |
|---|---|---|
| ramp-up time | 6-9 months | 6-8 weeks |
| calls to first appointment | 200+ | 80-120 |
| compliance violations | caught after the fact | caught live |
| coaching sessions per agent | 2/month | every call |

## Why this matters for insurance

insurance sales has **strict compliance requirements.**

one wrong phrase can trigger a regulatory issue. real-time monitoring catches problems before they become violations.

> "we went from reviewing 5% of calls to monitoring 100% of them."
