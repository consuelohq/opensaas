## What's actually working

not everything labeled "AI" delivers. here's what the data says matters:

### 1. real-time call coaching

[mckinsey found](https://convin.ai/blog/insurance-sales-automation) that insurers using AI coaching see **10-20% improvement in new-agent success rates** and sales conversion rates.

the difference from traditional coaching:

| traditional | AI-powered |
|---|---|
| 2 coaching sessions/month | every call coached |
| manager reviews 5% of calls | 100% of calls analyzed |
| feedback days later | feedback in real-time |
| generic advice | specific to the conversation |

### 2. claims processing speed

AI-powered claims tools have reduced resolution time from [14 days to 2.3 days](https://zipdo.co/ai-in-the-insurance-industry-statistics/) in property insurance.

that's not incremental improvement. that's a different business.

### 3. AI roleplay for training

the biggest shift in 2026: [AI sales roleplay](https://kendo.ai/blogs/best-insurance-sales-training-programs) has removed the risk from training entirely.

new agents practice objection handling, compliance scenarios, and product pitches against AI — before they ever talk to a real prospect.

> "we went from 6-month ramp-up to 6 weeks."

## What's NOT working

| approach | why it fails |
|---|---|
| chatbots replacing agents | customers still want humans for complex insurance decisions |
| AI-generated cold emails at scale | [2.7% average success rate](https://scrap.io/cold-calling-success-rate) — same as manual |
| "AI-powered" CRMs with no real AI | rebranded search features, not actual intelligence |
| generic AI tools | insurance has compliance requirements that general tools ignore |

## Where to start

the agencies getting results aren't buying 10 AI tools. they're picking **one or two** and going deep.

**if you have a coaching problem** → real-time call coaching
**if you have a volume problem** → AI-powered dialer
**if you have an onboarding problem** → AI roleplay training

> [78% of insurance leaders](https://openkoda.com/insurtech-trends/) plan to increase their technology budgets in 2026. the question isn't whether to adopt AI — it's which AI to adopt first.
