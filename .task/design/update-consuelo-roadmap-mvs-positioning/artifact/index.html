Executive Summary

The roadmap decision

Build the company around two product entry points, two focused first segments, and one category thesis: decision infrastructure. Consuelo Dialer starts with insurance teams. Consuelo OS starts with developers/operators building agent-ready company workflows.

  • Keep Consuelo OS and Consuelo Dialer active as separate products with separate buyers.
  • Anchor each product in a minimum viable segment before expanding.
  • Use decision infrastructure as the shared category story across both products.
  • Prioritize bottom-of-funnel distribution over generic awareness.
  • Use marketplace presence, insurance outreach, operator channels, and landing pages to turn positioning into qualified demand.

What changed

This roadmap turns the brain dump and follow-up research into an operating artifact. It keeps the StreamOS reader shell and spec layout, then sharpens product, growth, marketplace, pricing, and positioning work around the first segments each product can win.

Problem / Context

Consuelo has two products that need one clean growth story.

Consuelo OS and Consuelo Dialer serve different buying moments, but they point to the same market shift: business software is moving from storing context to deciding what should happen next.

The growth problem is distribution, clarity, and segment focus. The company needs landing pages, marketplace listings, outreach, and pricing hypotheses that make the two-product wedge understandable through concrete first buyers: insurance teams for Dialer and developers/operators for OS.

Users / Buyers / Jobs

The roadmap starts with two minimum viable segments.

Insurance team

First Dialer segment. Insurance teams already depend on outbound calling, follow-up, appointment setting, sales discipline, and rep coaching. Consuelo Dialer should decide who to call next, help the rep execute, log what happened, and trigger the right follow-up.

Developer / operator

First OS segment. Developers, technical founders, AI operators, and internal operations owners already understand the pain of giving agents safe access to company context, tools, files, workflows, approvals, and execution logs.

Insurance AI operator

Bridge segment. Insurance teams asking how to use AI better can start with the Dialer workflow, then expand into Consuelo OS as the company computer for calls, notes, follow-ups, tasks, coaching, reporting, and approved workflows.

Goals And Non-goals

Scope control

Goals

  • Make decision infrastructure the category spine.
  • Build separate landing pages for OS and Dialer.
  • Launch bottom-of-funnel marketplace distribution.
  • Run Meta and Google ads as support channels, not the whole strategy.
  • Turn insurance outreach and AI-native operator outreach into repeatable offers.

Non-goals

  • Do not choose between OS and Dialer.
  • Do not flatten both products into one vague platform page.
  • Do not run generic ads before landing-page positioning is tight.
  • Do not price OS compute until the hosted company-computer unit is clear.
  • Do not treat marketplaces as afterthoughts.

Product Design

Two products, two wedges, one shared category.

The clean model is not one blended product. It is a shared decision-infrastructure thesis with separate product packaging and focused first segments.

Category

Decision infrastructure: systems that use context to decide what should happen next and help execute it.

Dialer wedge

Insurance teams: who to call, what the rep should know, what happened, and what follow-up should fire next.

OS wedge

Developers/operators: company computer for context, tools, skills, files, workflows, approvals, memory, and agent execution.

Bridge

Insurance teams can expand from Dialer into OS when they need a broader AI operating layer over calls, notes, tasks, reporting, and workflows.

Marketplaces

Agent/operator surfaces for OS; CRM, sales, and insurance-adjacent surfaces for Dialer.

Expansion

Operator users become business champions; insurance Dialer accounts expand into broader Consuelo OS usage.

focused segment -> product-specific offer -> activated workspace -> self-proving value -> business expansion

Distribution Lanes

Show up where the first segments already have intent.

Insurance Dialer distribution

Primary product: Consuelo Dialer. Channels include insurance agency outreach, insurance sales communities, GoHighLevel, HubSpot where relevant, CRM marketplaces, sales-tool directories, telephony ecosystems, and insurance sales consultants.

Developer / operator OS distribution

Primary product: Consuelo OS. Channels include agent developer communities, Codex / Claude / OpenCode / ChatGPT operator ecosystems, automation consultants, AI ops newsletters, GitHub, local CLI demos, and technical docs.

Insurance AI workspace expansion

Primary product path: Dialer first, OS expansion second. Use real calling, follow-up, coaching, and reporting workflows to show how OS becomes the broader company computer after the Dialer earns trust.

Decision Log

Decisions that should not be re-litigated

Keep both products active.

OS and Dialer have different buyer profiles. The wedge is stronger when both products are sold through the shared decision-infrastructure thesis.

Lead with minimum viable segments.

Dialer starts with insurance teams. OS starts with developers/operators. This makes pain, budget, use case, channel, and proof more repeatable than broad persona targeting.

Prioritize bottom-of-funnel distribution.

Ads matter, but marketplace, ecosystem, community, and direct outreach surfaces should come first because those surfaces already contain buyer intent.

Position OS as the company computer.

For businesses, OS is the hosted environment where agents get the context, tools, storage, skills, workflows, permissions, and execution surface required to work productively.

Position Consuelo Dialer as the insurance outbound calling and follow-up system.

The dialer product is a revenue decision system. It should sell the operational outcome: better prioritization, calling, follow-up, coaching, and execution for insurance teams first.

Operators are the first OS champions.

Developers, technical founders, AI operators, and internal operations owners are most likely to understand the company-computer need, adopt OS personally, and bring it into business workflows.

Alternatives Considered

Rejected paths for the next slice

Pick only OS or only Dialer

Rejected. The two-product wedge is the advantage. Each product gives the category story a different buying surface.

Run generic brand ads first

Rejected. Paid campaigns should point at specific offers and buyer intent. Landing pages and marketplace listings come first.

Hide behind broad AI language

Rejected. The message should be operational: decide what should happen next, then execute it.

Risks

What can break or confuse the plan

Category blur

Risk: decision infrastructure sounds abstract. Mitigation: always anchor it to next-action workflows: who to call, what to do, what agent should execute.

Marketplace friction

Risk: listing requirements slow launches. Mitigation: score channels by friction, buyer intent, proof needed, and speed to first listing.

Unclear OS pricing

Risk: compute/storage/workflow pricing becomes too abstract. Mitigation: start with hosted company computer, workspace capacity, workflow runs, storage, skill updates, and agent tool execution.

Two-product confusion

Risk: buyers do not understand why OS and Dialer live together. Mitigation: category page explains the shared decision layer; product pages sell specific jobs.

Operating Cadence

Development cannot keep eating the marketing calendar.

Consuelo needs a weekly operating rhythm that protects marketing and distribution work with the same seriousness as product development. The company can stay lean, but it cannot stay invisible.

The practical rule: set aside a small weekly paid-acquisition budget and a fixed marketing block before deciding what to build next. Development should support the best distribution experiments, not replace them.

Open Questions

Questions to answer while building the next slice

What is the OS pricing unit?

Candidate units: hosted company computer, storage, workflows, skill updates, agent tool execution, and workspace capacity.

Which marketplace launches first?

Pick the channel with the best mix of listing speed, buyer intent, product fit, and proof requirements.

How should Dialer enter CRM stores?

Package as Dialer, not as a narrow calling add-on. The promise is outbound decision workflow.

What offer converts operators and insurance teams?

Start with two offers: help insurance teams improve calling/follow-up with AI-assisted workflows, and help operators make the company agent-ready with OS as the implementation layer.

2. Consuelo OS Packaging company computer

  • •Position Consuelo OS as the company computer for AI-native businesses.
  • Define the personal-user entry: 80% of technical setup handled, 20% personalization through the user's agent.
  • Define the business entry: hosted workspace with context, tools, skills, storage, workflows, permissions, and agent tool execution.

  • Map personal-user activation into business expansion: user adopts OS personally, then brings it into the company that hires them for AI-native work.

3. Consuelo Dialer Packaging Dialer

  • •Position Consuelo Dialer as the insurance outbound calling and follow-up system, not just a calling tool.
  • Clarify the core decision job: who to call next, when to call, what happened, and what workflow should fire after the call.
  • Write the Dialer landing page with insurance-team, sales-manager, agency, and CRM-store buyer language.
  • Define pricing units: seats, number pool access, minutes/credits, workflows, analytics, and coaching surfaces.
  • Create a dialer demo script that shows the decision workflow, not only the call mechanics.

4. Marketplace Scorecard intent surfaces

  • •Rank marketplaces by buyer intent, listing friction, proof required, integration effort, and speed to launch.

5. Marketplace Listing Packet assets

6. Landing Pages conversion surfaces

7. Paid Acquisition sponsorships + ads
